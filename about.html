Education plays a huge part of today’s society. Several consumers just don’t know that certain options are available to them. We plan to hold potential workshops in educating the general public and also providing talks at various schools. To apply change, we must educate people of all ages and backgrounds. Even producers need to be educated in regards to how their products are being disposed of, and we should hold them accountable. They play a part in also educating the consumer in proper methods of maintenance, recycling, and more.

Products used to be built to last. Now, they are built to be used every few years, then consumers get a replacement. Ask yourself, how often do you get a new phone? Why do you get a new phone? Is it because it broke, or is it because your phone becomes too slow, or because the company no longer supports that device? After answering and thinking about these questions, think about this. How do you dispose of your old phone? Do you throw it away? Do you sell it? Do you try to get it repaired? Very rarely it’s the latter. When consumers throw away their electronics, as an example this produces a lot of e-waste. Which contaminates drinking water and polluting ecosystems around the globe. Only a few electronics are actually recycled, and those that aren’t are put into landfills or burned in another country which enhances the problem. We need to get companies to build things that last, or educate their consumers in proper recycling or repairing techniques, and they should work with recycling companies to contribute to creating more recycling areas, and ensure that their items are being discarded in a safe and in an environmentally friendly way.

As an organization we will work to create right to repair laws allowing consumers to actually own the product that they buy. This will allow them for the right to use, to modify, and to repair. With these laws, everyone should be given the right to proper manuals from producers, the right to choose which service shop we use, and the right to look into the software on our personal devices. Manufacturers have created a monopoly on repair, for example Apple has created special screws for their devices making it even harder to repair them at a service shop. Several companies drive prices up and drive down competition for themself, we should not give them that option! It’s obvious that numerous companies don’t want people to repair their items. Many companies refuse to sell replacement parts to independent service shops, which is very unfair. There are various repair cafes and numerous people to help, however, we as an organization need to give pressure in applying right to repair laws.
